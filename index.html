Welcome to Tulunadu

Tulunadu, located along the picturesque southwestern coast of India, is a land where the Arabian Sea meets lush green landscapes. Known for its vibrant festivals, unique Tulu language, and centuries-old traditions, Tulunadu is a cultural treasure trove that blends history, spirituality, and natureâ€™s beauty.

History

The roots of Tulunadu trace back over 2,000 years, with references in ancient inscriptions and Sangam literature. It was a thriving hub for maritime trade, connecting India to the Arab world and beyond. Rich in temple architecture and folklore, it preserves a unique heritage that is distinct from neighboring regions.

Language

The native tongue of Tulunadu is Tulu, a Dravidian language spoken by over two million people. Tulu has its own script (Tigalari), though it is now mostly written in Kannada script. Despite being a spoken language for daily life, it has a rich oral tradition of songs, proverbs, and riddles.
